<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; A Marriage &amp;
Parenthood (M&amp;P) Reset Workgroup has been set up to develop a whole-of-society
approach to better support Singaporeans in their M&amp;P journeys.</p>
<p>2&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; In 2025, Singapore’s
Total Fertility Rate fell to a new low of 0.87 from 0.97 the year before.
M&amp;P aspirations and outcomes are shaped by social attitudes that influence
life priorities and choices, as well as enabling conditions such as Government
policies, workplace practices, and family and community support. Addressing
Singapore’s declining fertility will require a multi-faceted, whole-of-society
reset.</p>
<p>3&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; To address this
challenge, Ms Indranee Rajah, <strong>Minister in the Prime Minister’s Office, Second Minister for Finance and Second Minister for National Development</strong>,
will chair a new interagency Workgroup with Political Office Holders from
relevant ministries. The other Workgroup members are:</p>

<p>4&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; The Workgroup will focus
on three areas to realise a collaborative, whole-of-society M&amp;P reset.
It will:</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;a)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Develop a long-term roadmap</strong> that
sets out the shifts needed to bring about a societal reset on how M&amp;P
is perceived, supported and realised in Singapore;</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;b)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Propose M&amp;P policy enhancements</strong> to
address key concerns and enable these shifts; and</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;c)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Mobilise the whole of society</strong> to
collectively support Singaporeans in their M&amp;P journeys, as a shared
national endeavour.</p>
<p>5&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Workgroup will
examine key factors that influence M&amp;P decisions including financial
costs, work-life support, caregiving, housing, healthcare, preschool and
education. It will also work with stakeholders to promote societal norms
and workplace practices that better support M&amp;P aspirations.</p>
<p>6&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; The Workgroup
– supported by the National Population and Talent Division – will engage
widely to gather ideas and feedback on policies, as well as to secure the
commitment of stakeholders – employers, businesses, community organisations,
and individual Singaporeans – to contribute to this collective effort.</p>
<p>7&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; The Workgroup
aims to release a full report with consolidated findings early next year
though it may also make early recommendations where appropriate prior to
the issuance of the full report.</p>
